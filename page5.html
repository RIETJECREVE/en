Europen ceramics Workcenter

On monday february 5 we left our atelier and went to the EKWC to learn to print with a ceramic 3D printer.

Europen Ceramics Workcenter or EKWC sunday morning is an atelier were different artists from different countries  can work  for six months.  The people who work there sleep in little rooms and they start every day of their six months working with art and technology.

3D - scan
Fablap

On the first day we started with 3D scanning. First we had to find an object from our environment that we wanted to scan. I had chosen a small plant because I was curious about what would come out of the 3D printer.

Z-Printer

Step 2 was to convert the plant and improve it in the fablap 3D program. I chose to fill the form so that it had few open spaces. During the work process it became clear that the object had too little mass to print and we chose to print the plant with a different printer. The Z-printer is a machine that with a powder layer overlay only the drawing is sprayed with a kind of liquid. So that it becomes hard after the different layers.

3D - print
porcelain

On the third day I had made a new design that was possible to print. The idea was to print a rotated cylinder. The idea is that the form can only be realized with a computer and machine. I was curious about the result. First the machine starts with a grid-shaped bottom and after a while the porcelain starts to stack.

Design 

After half an hour's waiting, the design began to come into being. I was faded that it still went fast. If the figure was 5cm high, the drying process had to be accelerated, otherwise it could collapse due to the heaviness. It was a nice experience
